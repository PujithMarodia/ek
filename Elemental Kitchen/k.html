Potassium

Potassium is a chemical element with symbol K (from Neo-Latin kalium) and atomic number 19. Freshly cut potassium is silvery in appearance, but it begins to tarnish toward gray immediately on exposure to air. Potassium is widely used in fertilizers, medicines, food additives and more.

Symbol: K

Atomic number: 19

Atomic mass: 39.0983 u

Element category: Alkali metal

Group: group 1

Fun Facts!

1. Potassium is the 7th most abundant element in the Earth's crust, accounting for about 2.5% of its mass.

2. Potassium is the second lightest (least dense) metal after lithium.

3. Potassium was first purified in 1807 by Sir Humphry Davy from caustic potash (KOH) via electrolysis. Potassium was the first metal to be isolated using electrolysis.

Sodium

Sodium is a chemical element with symbol Na (from Latin natrium) and atomic number 11. It is a soft, silvery-white, highly reactive metal. The most common compound of sodium is sodium chloride (common salt). It is added to food and used to de-ice roads in winter. It is also used as a feedstock for the chemical industry. Sodium carbonate (washing soda) is also a useful sodium salt.

Symbol: Na

Atomic number: 11

Atomic mass: 22.989769 u

Element category: Alkali metal

Group: group 1

Fun Facts!

1. It is the sixth most abundant metal in the Earth's crust.

2. Sodium was first isolated from sodium hydroxide by Humphry Davy in 1807.

3. It has been used as a form of currency, medical treatments, and trade product for thousands of years.
